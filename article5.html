History and Theme of International Sign Language Day 2024

23 September 2024

International Sign Languages Day, celebrated annually on September 23rd, serves as a crucial opportunity to acknowledge and support the unique linguistic identities and cultural richness of deaf and hard-of-hearing individuals. According to the World Federation of the Deaf (WFD), over 70 million people worldwide experience deafness, with 80% residing in developing countries. This diverse population utilizes a staggering 300 distinct sign languages for communication.

The United Nations recognizes sign languages as fully developed natural languages, with structures independent of spoken languages. To emphasize the importance of sign languages in ensuring the human rights of deaf individuals, the UN General Assembly proclaimed September 23rd as International Sign Languages Day. This resolution specifically highlights the need for early access to quality sign language services, including education.

Looking at the history of this important day, International Sign Languages Day was established by the WFD, an organization representing approximately 70 million deaf people globally. The resolution for its official recognition, A/RES/72/161, was championed by Antigua and Barbuda's Permanent Mission to the United Nations and garnered support from 97 member states, leading to its unanimous adoption on December 19th, 2017.

The selection of September 23rd as International Sign Languages Day coincides with the founding date of the WFD in 1951. This date symbolizes the birth of a significant advocacy organization dedicated to preserving sign languages and deaf culture. These elements are recognized as fundamental prerequisites for securing deaf people's human rights.

The first official celebration of International Sign Languages Day took place in 2018, integrated within the broader framework of International Week of the Deaf. Notably, International Deaf Week was first observed in September 1958 and has since evolved into a global movement promoting deaf unity and raising awareness about challenges faced by deaf individuals in their daily lives.

The 2024 celebration of International Sign Languages Day calls for the international community to come together under the unifying banner of sign language. World leaders and officials are encouraged to endorse the theme "Sign Up for Sign Language Rights." The global community strives towards upholding the linguistic human rights of deaf people, working alongside the implementation of the Rights of Persons with Disabilities convention, adopted in December 2006. In line with the spirit of inclusivity, blue remains the primary color for the 2024 celebration of International Sign Languages Day. This vibrant blue reaffirms a shared commitment to empowering national sign languages and expressing solidarity with the global deaf community.
