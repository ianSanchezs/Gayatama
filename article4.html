Get to Know Sign Language for The Deaf

23 September 2024

International Sign Language Day, commemorated on September 23, serves as a recognition of the vibrant and diverse world of sign languages used by the global deaf community. According to the World Federation of the Deaf, the number of deaf people worldwide has surpassed 70 million, each utilizing one of over 300 distinct sign languages.

Sign languages, while structurally different from spoken languages, are complete natural languages in their own right. In Indonesia, deaf individuals employ two primary sign languages: the Indonesian Sign Language System (SIBI) and Indonesian Sign Language (Bisindo). SIBI, officially recognized by the Indonesian government, serves as the instructional language in Special Needs Schools (SLB) for deaf children. It aims to visually represent the grammatical structure of Indonesian spoken language. On the other hand, Bisindo emerged organically within Indonesian culture and is widely used by the deaf community in daily life. It was developed by a group of deaf individuals based on their observations and experiences.

As emphasized by Kustinah, a teacher at Tulungagung Special Needs School, learning sign language can be facilitated through the use of songs. She suggests starting with recognizing the alphabet and then progressing to identifying objects and family members. This approach can make the learning process more engaging and effective.

Beyond its significance as a means of communication, International Sign Language Day also serves as a reminder of the importance of hearing health. Dr. M. Mundir Arif, an ENT specialist at dr. Iskak Hospital, urges everyone to prioritize hearing care. He highlights the potential consequences of hearing loss and underscores the crucial role of sign language in enabling effective communication and learning for those who are deaf.
